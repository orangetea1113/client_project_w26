Networking Support

The Power of Professional Connections

Networking is more than just exchanging business cards; it is a powerful tool for career development that opens doors to new opportunities, insights, and relationships. Whether you are new to networking or looking to refine your skills, building a robust professional network is essential for long-term career success.

As a UMSI student, you have access to a wealth of resources and a vibrant community eager to support your journey. This page provides practical tips, strategies, and resources to help you connect effectively with professionals in your field.

Building Your Networking Strategy

A successful networking strategy involves more than just reaching out to people; it requires thoughtful planning and genuine engagement. Here are some tips and techniques to help you build and maintain meaningful professional relationships:

Networking Do's & Don'ts

When seeking to build professional relationships, keep these golden rules in mind:

DO DON'T
Keep it short: Aim for under 100 words. Never ask for a job: Build the relationship first.
Establish a time frame: Ask for a specific 20-minute slot. Don't send your resume first: Only send it if asked.
Personalize: Show you've done your research. Don't ask Google-able questions: Ask for insights, not facts.

How to Reach Out: The 5-Point Email

Effective networking emails should follow this 5-point structure:

  1. The Hook: Mention your connection first (e.g., "As a fellow UMSI student...").
  2. The Why: Generalize your interest (e.g., "I am interested in the work you are doing at Google...").
  3. The Ask: Keep it low pressure (e.g., "I'd love to ask you a few questions...").
  4. The Constraint: Define the time (e.g., "Would you be open to a 20-minute chat?").
  5. The Control: Handle the scheduling (e.g., "I can work around your availability").

What to Ask: The TIARA Method

Once you get the meeting, use the TIARA framework to structure your questions. This moves the conversation from small talk to deep insights.

  • T - Trends: "What trends are impacting your business right now?"
  • I - Insights: "What surprises you most about your current role?"
  • A - Advice: "What can I do right now to prepare for a career in this field?"
  • R - Resources: "What resources (blogs, podcasts) should I be following?"
  • A - Assignments: "What kind of projects add the most value to your team?"

Networking FAQ

  • How do I follow up after a networking meeting?
    Send a thank-you email within 24 hours, mentioning specific points from your conversation and expressing your appreciation for their time.
  • What if I don't get a response to my initial outreach?
    It's okay to send one polite follow-up email after a week or two. If you still don't hear back, respect their time and consider reaching out to someone else.
  • How can I maintain my network over time?
    Stay in touch by sharing relevant articles, congratulating them on achievements, or checking in periodically without asking for favors.
