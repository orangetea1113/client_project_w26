Mastering the Interview: Your Path to Success

Interviews are a critical step in the job search process, providing an opportunity to showcase your skills, experiences, and fit for a role. Whether you're preparing for your first interview or looking to refine your technique, this page offers valuable resources and tips to help you succeed.

As a UMSI student, you have access to tailored support and guidance to help you navigate interviews with confidence. Explore the sections below for strategies, common questions, and additional resources to enhance your interview skills.

Building Your Interview Skills

Successful interviews require preparation, practice, and a positive mindset. Here are some key strategies to help you excel in your interviews:

The Golden Rule: The STAR Method

When answering behavioral questions (e.g., "Tell me about a time you failed"), use the STAR Method to structure a clear, compelling story.

  • S - Situation: Briefly set the scene (Who, when, where?).
  • T - Task: What was the problem or goal you were working toward?
  • A - Action: What did YOU specifically do? (Use "I" statements, not "We").
  • R - Result: What was the outcome? Quantify it if possible (e.g., "Improved efficiency by 20%").

Ace the Virtual Interview

Most initial screens and tech interviews happen remotely. Treat your setup as part of your professional appearance.

  • Eye Contact: Look at the camera lens, not the screen, when you are speaking.
  • Lighting: Face a window or light source. Avoid strong backlighting (which makes you look like a shadow).
  • Tech Check: Test your microphone and internet connection 10 minutes before the call.
  • Digital Background: If your room is messy, use a blurred background or a professional virtual background.

Interview FAQ

  • How should I prepare for an interview?
    Research the company, review the job description, practice common questions, and prepare your own questions to ask.
  • What should I wear to an interview?
    Dress appropriately for the company culture. When in doubt, it's better to be slightly overdressed than underdressed.
  • How do I handle difficult questions?
    Stay calm, take a moment to think, and use the STAR method to structure your response.
  • What questions should I ask the interviewer?
    Ask about team dynamics, company culture, growth opportunities, and next steps in the hiring process.
